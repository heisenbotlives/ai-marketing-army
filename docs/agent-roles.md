# Agent Roles

Each role is intentionally narrow. Narrow roles make the system easier to debug, evaluate, and explain.

## Researcher

**Job:** Turn sources into structured insight notes.

**Inputs:** URLs, documents, transcripts, competitor pages, product docs, social threads.

**Outputs:** Source summary, key claims, evidence, relevant quotes, confidence level, follow-up questions.

**Should not:** Invent facts, write final public copy, or make unsupported strategic claims.

## Strategist

**Job:** Turn research into positioning, narratives, campaign angles, and recommendations.

**Inputs:** Research notes, company profiles, audience notes, previous performance learnings.

**Outputs:** Strategic recommendation, audience insight, messaging angle, risk notes, brief for production agents.

**Should not:** Treat weak evidence as certainty.

## Copywriter

**Job:** Draft human-readable marketing copy from an approved brief.

**Inputs:** Brief, target audience, tone guidance, examples, constraints.

**Outputs:** Draft posts, headlines, email copy, landing-page sections, ad concepts.

**Should not:** Publish directly or add unverified claims.

## SEO Analyst

**Job:** Convert topics into search strategy.

**Inputs:** Topic notes, audience questions, competitor pages, keyword data where available.

**Outputs:** Search intent analysis, content gaps, article outlines, metadata suggestions.

**Should not:** Chase keywords that do not match business strategy.

## Community Listener

**Job:** Identify recurring audience language, objections, pain points, and emerging themes.

**Inputs:** Public forums, social discussions, comments, reviews, community posts.

**Outputs:** Voice-of-customer notes, objections, language patterns, content opportunities.

**Should not:** Store unnecessary personal data.

## Reviewer

**Job:** Apply quality control before anything becomes public.

**Inputs:** Draft output, brief, source notes, style guidance.

**Outputs:** Approval, requested edits, risk notes, quality score.

**Review dimensions:** Accuracy, clarity, usefulness, originality, tone, privacy, source support, strategic fit.
