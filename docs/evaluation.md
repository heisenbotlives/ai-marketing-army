# Evaluation

AI marketing systems need quality gates. Speed is only useful if the output is accurate, useful, safe, and on strategy.

## Review decisions

Every customer-facing or decision-influencing output should end with one of four decisions:

- **Approve:** Safe to use after human review
- **Revise:** Useful direction, but needs changes
- **Reject:** Not accurate, useful, or strategically relevant
- **Escalate:** Needs human, legal, compliance, or domain-expert judgement

## Output scorecard

Score each dimension from 1 to 5:

- **Accuracy:** Are claims supported by evidence?
- **Clarity:** Is it easy to understand?
- **Usefulness:** Does it help the intended audience?
- **Strategic fit:** Does it support the positioning or campaign goal?
- **Originality:** Does it avoid generic AI-sounding copy?
- **Tone:** Does it match the brand and channel?
- **Actionability:** Is there a clear next step or decision?
- **Risk:** Are there privacy, compliance, reputational, or platform concerns?

## Agent-specific checks

### Research

- Sources are listed
- Claims are separated from interpretation
- Date/context is captured
- Confidence level is stated
- Open questions are listed

### Performance Analyst

- Data source and date are listed
- Correlation is not overstated as causation
- Recommendation includes measurement criteria
- Caveats are explicit

### Content

- Claims are source-backed
- Draft follows brand voice
- Draft has a clear audience and point of view
- Draft does not sound generic or overproduced

### Social & Community

- Channel format fits the platform
- Sensitive community details are not exposed
- No deceptive impersonation
- Human approval required before posting

### SEO

- Search intent is clear
- Recommendations fit business strategy
- No fabricated keyword volumes
- Internal-link suggestions are relevant

## Minimum approval standard

An output should not be approved if it has:

- Unsourced factual claims
- Private or sensitive information
- A misleading implication
- Off-brand tone
- No clear connection to the brief
